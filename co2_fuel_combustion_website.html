CO₂ Emissions from Fuel Combustion in Austria

This website presents current CO₂ emissions from the combustion of liquid and gaseous fuels within the energy sector. The calculations presented are rough calculations using monthly fuel combustion activities and should present similar results to the CO₂ emissions from fuel combustion published in Austria's National Inventory Document.

CO₂ emissions from these three categories are presented: Natural Gas, Oil Products and International Aviation.

Emissions from natural gas are calculated from gas consumption data published by E-Control. The data is modified by subtracting gas used for non-energy purposes.

Emissions from oil products are calculated from consumption data published by Eurostat. The fuel types gasoil and dieseloil, fuel oil, liquified petroleum gas (LPG), gasolin and kerosene are used. Most of the kerosene, however, is accounted towards international aviation. To include missing consumption activities in the raw data, constant monthly CO₂ emissions of 240 tons are added.

CO₂ emissions from international aviation are calculated from 98.83% of kerosene consumption published in the dataset from Eurostat.

To receive more detailed information about the calculations, the code can be seen in a GitHub Repository . This project is part of the bachelor thesis from Elijah Stängl. A complete documentation of the methodology is available in the bachelor thesis available online.

The category "Total" consists of the sum over all calculated CO₂ emissions from Oil Products, Natural Gas and International Aviation. It is important to mention that International Aviation is normally not accounted towards the emissions of the given country.
